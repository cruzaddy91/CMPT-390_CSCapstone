# Capstone Presentation Information and Rubric (Apr 24, 2026)

## General Information

- Presentations run from **9:00 am to 12:30 pm** on **Friday, April 24**.
- Bagels are served at the beginning.
- Students should attend presentations during their time block and support classmates.

## Presentation Timing Expectations

- Total presentation target: **10 minutes**, plus up to **5 minutes Q&A**.
- Warning is given at **9 minutes**.
- **Exemplary time frame:** **9-10 minutes**.
- **Meets Expectations time frame:** **6-11 minutes** (still aim for 10).

## Required 10-Minute Content

Your presentation should include:

1. Introduction, including post-graduation plans
2. Project goals (connected to post-graduation plans if applicable)
3. Technology/libraries used and why
4. Accomplishments or discoveries, including lessons learned
5. Short project demo (ideally a recorded video)
6. Conclusion summarizing 1-2 main ideas

Additional guidance:

- Most presenters should avoid showing code.
- Prioritize approximately **two big ideas** the audience should remember.

## Grading Structure

Presentation contributes to both:

- **Capstone Project (CP) assignment grade**
- **Professional Development (PD) assignment grade**

### CP assignment grade conditions

- **Exemplary CP:** Exemplary in all 5 CP criteria + Meets (or better) in 4+ PD criteria
- **Meets CP:** Meets (or better) in 4+ CP criteria + Meets (or better) in 2+ PD criteria

### PD assignment grade conditions

- **Exemplary PD:** Exemplary in all 5 PD criteria + Meets (or better) in 4+ CP criteria
- **Meets PD:** Meets (or better) in all 5 PD criteria + Meets (or better) in 3+ CP criteria

## CP Criteria Rubric

### 1) Project Overview and Goals

- **Requires Revision:** Purpose unclear; project context/goals not clear
- **Meets:** Context and goals described; audience understands goals and relevance
- **Exemplary:** Audience clearly understands goals, why interesting, and larger context

### 2) Time Frame

- **Requires Revision:** Presentation outside minimum/maximum window
- **Meets:** Between 6 and 11 minutes
- **Exemplary:** Between 9 and 10 minutes

### 3) Accomplishments / Subject Knowledge

- **Requires Revision:** Accomplishments unclear or weak; roadblocks dominated; weak question handling
- **Meets:** Significant accomplishments tied to goals; roadblocks noted; answers most questions satisfactorily
- **Exemplary:** Impressive accomplishments; roadblocks overcome; thorough knowledge with elaborated answers

### 4) Technical Choices and Lessons Learned

- **Requires Revision:** Does not explain technologies used and/or why
- **Meets:** Notes technologies/tools used and why
- **Exemplary:** Clearly explains tool/technology choices and shares lessons learned

### 5) Demo / Results

- **Requires Revision:** Results not demonstrated
- **Meets:** Screenshots/video shown; demo may be less polished
- **Exemplary:** Strong results clearly and concisely demonstrated with a recorded video

## PD Criteria Rubric

### 1) Introduction

- **Requires Revision:** No introduction or no post-graduation plans
- **Meets:** Clear introduction including name and plans
- **Exemplary:** Strong introduction with 1-2 sentences about presenter background

### 2) Presentation Organization

- **Requires Revision:** Lacks clear structure; abrupt transitions
- **Meets:** Thoughts clear; some organization issues
- **Exemplary:** Well organized, logical flow, smooth transitions

### 3) Engagement and Delivery

- **Requires Revision:** Minimal eye contact or hard to hear/understand
- **Meets:** Clear speech, professional language/tone, thoughtful Q&A handling
- **Exemplary:** Enthusiastic/confident, maintains audience engagement, appropriately dressed

### 4) Visual Aids

- **Requires Revision:** Missing/poor visuals; unreadable text
- **Meets:** Visual aids present but could be more professional
- **Exemplary:** Professional, visually appealing aids that complement presentation

### 5) Conclusion

- **Requires Revision:** Missing conclusion
- **Meets:** Includes conclusion
- **Exemplary:** Strong ending with summary of key points
