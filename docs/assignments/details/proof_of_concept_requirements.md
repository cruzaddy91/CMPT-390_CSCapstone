# Proof of Concept

Your "proof of concept" is a demonstration that the concept behind your project idea is possible.

This means different things regarding each project, but some examples include:

- You can communicate with a necessary API.
- Your application can communicate with a database.
- The hardware you require can communicate with software.
- You have the necessary machine learning libraries available and a plan to train your data set (which is also available).
- You are able to install the necessary software on your system.

Perhaps the easiest way to answer what is your proof of concept is to answer the simple question: **"What is your biggest concern or unknown about your project?"** Come up with a simple way to demonstrate that this concern or unknown is possible. We ask you to complete a proof of concept so as to avoid surprises in the spring semester.

## Deliverable

You will write up a **1 to 2 page summary** behind your proof of concept which describes why you believe all the details that are necessary for the successful completion of your project will work as expected. You can include screenshots or anything else that helps communicate this.

**Alternative:** Instead of writing up a proof of concept, you are also welcome to **demo your proof of concept to the instructor**. If you choose to do this, please arrange a mutually convenient meeting time.
